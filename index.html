Visit Project

 Using clay made from the fibers of newspapers as the primary material, Recompositions aims to explore the current state of information, data, media, and their respective institutions. It is a fallen structure created in response to a growing shift in the re-decentralization of the internet and the methods in which information and data are distributed and organized. It hopes to capture the poetry of the moments following the collapse of a monument, ready to be decomposed, recycled, and then reconstructed once more.

About

 Hi there! I'm Aldon and I'm comfortable with any pronouns. I was born in San Francisco and raised all around the Bay Area which unsurprisingly led to my ongoing fascination with cutting edge technology. Combined with an early affinity for fine art and design, it seemed natural for me to explore the intersection between the three fields. I grew up jailbreaking and tweaking my iPhone, designing everything from bags to speculative technology to video games, and of course, a lot of drawing. Today, I'm particularly interested in designing and developing interfaces which includes not only those found in digital products, but also that of interactive art, experimental writing, physical products, and everything in between.
 I have special places in my heart for many things: low-tech, the Minecraft soundtrack, decentralized technology, and experimental electronic music among other things. When I'm not working on a project I'm most likely spending time with my family, thrift shopping with a friend, or having a picnic at a nearby park.
